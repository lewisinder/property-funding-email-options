How the fund works
A PIE structure with lending secured against New Zealand property.
The Property Funding Mortgage Trust is structured as a Portfolio Investment Entity in New Zealand. The Fund invests in loans secured by first mortgages over residential, commercial and rural properties, as well as cash and term deposits with registered banks.
Independent oversight
The Fund is supervised by Public Trust, with assets held by Property Funding Custodians Limited, a subsidiary of Public Trust.
Conservative lending
Loans are required to be secured by first mortgages, with Loan to Value Ratios less than 60%.
Built-in buffers
A reserve fund is targeted up to 1% of Fund value, with a liquidity policy requiring 5% to 25% in cash and term deposits.
Who we lend to
Approved borrowers needing short to medium term property-backed funding across New Zealand.
Investment terms at a glance
Asset mix: residential 0-75%, commercial 0-25%, rural 0-65%, cash/TDs 5-25%.
Unit price: calculated quarterly and generally expected to remain at $1.00 unless significant losses exceed reserves.
Manager's fee: 2.45%, with no contribution, withdrawal or establishment fees for investors.
Withdrawals: minimum investment period of 3 months; requests are processed at the end of each quarter.
