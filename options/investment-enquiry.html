How it works
About Property Funding
Property Funding has specialised in first-mortgage lending for over 25 years through our wholesale and eligible-investor lending structures. In 2026 we established the Property Funding Mortgage Trust to make this strategy available through a registered Managed Investment Scheme.
We invest in loans secured by first mortgages over residential, commercial and rural properties throughout New Zealand. We also invest in cash and term deposits with registered Banks.
How a PIE works
The Property Funding Mortgage Trust is a Managed Investment Scheme structured as a Portfolio Investment Entity (PIE) in New Zealand.
The investment objective of the Fund is to provide investors with an income stream which is higher than bank deposits and which is competitive with returns offered by other Managed Investment Schemes investing in first mortgage loans.

Quick definitions
Key investment terms
MIS
Managed Investment Scheme
A pooled investment where investors’ money is combined and managed by a manager. Returns depend on the Manager’s investment decisions and the performance of the investments.
1ST
First Mortgage
The primary mortgage security over a property. The Fund lends money secured by first mortgages, meaning it has first-ranking security over the property compared with later lenders.
UNIT
Units
The interests investors receive in the Fund when they invest. Instead of owning individual loans, investors hold Units in the Fund.
PAY
Distributions
Monthly payments intended to be made to investors from income earned by the Fund, after tax, fees, expenses and Reserve Fund contributions.

Conservative lending
Loans are required to be secured by first mortgages, with Loan to Value Ratios (LVRs) less than 60%, diversified across residential, commercial and rural properties.
Built-in buffers
We operate a reserve fund which we aim to keep at a level up to 1% of the overall Fund value. We also have a liquidity policy requiring between 5% - 25% of the Fund to be held in cash and term deposits.
Who we lend to
We lend to approved borrowers who require short to medium term funding for property backed purposes.
These include purchasing/refinancing residential, commercial or rural property, working capital for business purposes and property development (with controls such as staged drawdowns). Typical loan terms are 6-12 months.

Frequently asked questions
What are the fees?
The total fund charge is estimated to be 2.70%, which is comprised of 2.45% for the manager's fee and 0.25% for administration fee and charges which is primarily supervisor, registry and audit fees. There are no entry or exit fees charged to investors.
What is the Fund's objective?
The investment objective of the Fund is to provide investors with an income stream which is higher than bank deposits and which is competitive with returns offered by other Managed Investment Schemes investing in first mortgage loans.
Is there a reserve fund?
Yes - the reserve fund will be up to 1% of the Fund's value. The reserve fund is designed to mitigate against a fall in distributions as a result of defaults by borrowers or other events affecting investment returns. The reserve fund may not be sufficient to offset losses in all situations and therefore, is not a guarantee against falls in distributions or Unit Value.
Is there a minimum investment amount?
Yes, the minimum investment amount is $50,000 and increments of $50,000 thereafter.
How do I increase my investment?
To increase your investment, complete a Notice of Subsequent Deposit, return it to us and deposit the money into the Property Funding bank account. Download this form from our website under 'Application Forms' or contact us for a copy. You can increase your investment as often as you like.
How do I get a tax certificate?
As a PIE fund, we will pay your tax directly to the IRD. Every year in May we will send you an investor statement with a summary of the distribution and tax paid.

What are the Risks?
All investments involve risk.

With any investment there is a chance the return may be different from what is expected. The key to our success has been the first-mortgage investment security of our lending (via first mortgages on New Zealand land and buildings with relatively conservative loan to valuation ratios).

This is further supported by the lending process we have elected to have in place before any investor funds can be placed with borrowers.

Our appointed supervisors add another layer of oversight, providing our investors with confidence that their money is in good hands and only being invested in accordance with the Trust Deed.

For more information on risk, please refer to the Product Disclosure Statement.
How does the biometrics facial recognition technology work?
Biometrics is facial recognition technology which biometrically matches your face to the picture on your ID. We do this to meet our Anti Money Laundering (AML) obligations. In order for us to do this you will be sent a link via a text message.

Using a mobile device you will be asked to: take a picture of your NZ driver licence or your NZ passport; follow prompts to take a video of your face; verify that the details captured off your NZ driver licence or NZ passport are correct; and confirm, amend or add your residential address.

We think facial recognition is the fastest and easiest way for you to confirm your identity and for us to identify you. But we do have other options available if you are unable to use the biometrics technology, or you don't have a smartphone. We can accept certified copies of identification and other documents, or you can contact us to discuss alternative options.
Why must we provide information about our Source of Funds?
Legally, we must comply with the AML/CFT Act, which helps protect our communities from criminal activity. This means that we need to collect and verify information about your identity, address, nature and purpose of the business relationship, and in some circumstances also your source of funds and wealth.
